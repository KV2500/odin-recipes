Samosa

Description:

Samosas are deep-fried, triangular pastries filled with a spiced mixture of potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat. They have a crispy, golden-brown exterior and are often served with chutneys for extra flavor.

If samosas were a data structure, they’d be a stack—layers of crispy goodness stacked on top of a spicy, well-structured filling. You pop one, and just like a recursive function, you find yourself calling another. Their triangular shape is basically a pointer to happiness, directing taste buds straight to satisfaction. The outer crust? A perfectly compiled shield, encapsulating a rich core of mashed potatoes, peas, and a mix of Indian spices that run a tight algorithm of flavors. And don’t even get me started on the chutneys—they’re like APIs that enhance the whole experience, making every bite an integration of sweet, spicy, and tangy notes. Debugging hunger with samosas? Always a successful execution.

Ingrediants:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • ½ cup green peas (boiled)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)

Steps to make Samosas:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Mix flour, salt, and oil. Add water gradually to form a firm dough. Let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the Filling: Heat oil, add cumin seeds, and sauté with ginger-garlic paste. Add mashed potatoes, peas, and spices. Mix well and let it cool.
  3. Shape the Samosas: Roll out the dough into thin circles, cut in half, and form a cone. Fill with the prepared mixture.
  4. Seal the Edges: Apply water along the edges and press to seal the samosa completely.
  5. Fry the Samosas: Heat oil and deep-fry on medium heat until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve: Enjoy hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.
